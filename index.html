Formula Guide

Complete Formula Reference

Understand exactly how each metric is calculated and what the numbers mean for your business.

ROI — Return on Investment

ROI = ((Revenue − Investment) ÷ Investment) × 100

Measures the overall profitability of an investment relative to its cost.

Below 0%Loss — Losing Money
0% – 100%Marginal — Low Profit
100% – 300%Good — Profitable
300%+Excellent — High Profit

ROAS — Return on Ad Spend

ROAS = Revenue from Ads ÷ Total Ad Spend

Shows how much revenue you earn for every dollar spent on advertising.

Below 1xLoss — Negative Return
1x – 3xMarginal — Barely Profitable
3x – 5xGood — Industry Average
5x+Excellent — High Efficiency

CPA — Cost Per Acquisition

CPA = Total Ad Spend ÷ Number of Conversions

The average cost to acquire one customer, lead, or conversion. Lower is better.

Very LowExcellent Efficiency
LowGood Campaign Performance
HighNeeds Optimization
Very HighCampaign Not Profitable

CPM — Cost Per Mille

CPM = (Total Ad Spend ÷ Total Impressions) × 1,000

The cost for 1,000 ad impressions. Used to compare media buying efficiency.

$0 – $5Excellent — Very Cheap Reach
$5 – $15Good — Average Display CPM
$15 – $30Moderate — Social/Video
$30+High — Premium Placement

CTR — Click-Through Rate

CTR = (Total Clicks ÷ Total Impressions) × 100

Percentage of people who clicked your ad. Higher CTR = more compelling ad creative.

Below 0.5%Poor — Ad Needs Work
0.5% – 1%Average — Room to Improve
1% – 3%Good — Strong Creative
3%+Excellent — Top Performer

Break-even Analysis

Break-even = Fixed Costs ÷ (Selling Price − Variable Cost)

The exact number of units you must sell to cover all costs — before making any profit.

Low UnitsBusiness Model is Strong
Medium UnitsViable but Watch Margins
High UnitsHigh Risk — Review Pricing

Profit Margins — Gross · Operating · Net

Gross Margin: ((Revenue − COGS) ÷ Revenue) × 100
Operating Margin: ((Revenue − COGS − Operating Expenses) ÷ Revenue) × 100
Net Margin: ((Revenue − All Costs) ÷ Revenue) × 100

Three layers of profitability: Gross shows production efficiency, Operating shows operational efficiency, Net shows overall profitability.

Below 5%Poor
5% – 15%Average
15% – 30%Good
30%+Excellent
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

ROI (Return on Investment) considers all costs including production, overhead, and ad spend, giving you overall profitability. ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) only looks at revenue vs. ad spend, making it specific to advertising efficiency. A campaign can have a great ROAS but a poor ROI if other costs are high.

Industry standard is 4x ROAS as a baseline. For e-commerce, 3x–5x is common. For high-margin products, 2x–3x may be profitable. For low-margin products, you may need 6x–10x+. Always calculate based on your specific profit margins.

To reduce CPA: (1) Improve ad creative and copy to increase CTR, (2) Refine audience targeting to reach more qualified prospects, (3) Optimize landing pages for better conversion rates, (4) Use retargeting to reach warm audiences, (5) Test different bidding strategies, (6) Pause underperforming ad sets and scale winners.
